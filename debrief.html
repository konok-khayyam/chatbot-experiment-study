Debriefing

Thank you for taking part in this study.

This study examines how different chatbot interaction styles influence people’s responses to manipulative or extremist-style online content.

The video you watched was fictional and created for this study. It was designed to simulate a one-sided persuasive message. The study does not endorse the views expressed in the video.

Some online extremist or manipulative messages use grievance, identity threat, urgency, dehumanisation, and claims that harmful action is necessary or justified.

The purpose of this study is to understand whether chatbot-based responses can reduce the influence of such messages and support more reflective engagement.

Participants were randomly assigned to different versions of the video and chatbot interaction. These versions differed in friendliness, conversational strategy, and empathy.
